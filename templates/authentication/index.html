How it Works

1

OCR Extraction

Scans handwritten or printed scripts with high accuracy.

2

Medical NLP

The Django backend processes abbreviations via medical dictionary.

3

Interaction & Safety Scan

The engine cross-references the medication against a database of known contraindications, flagging "Look-Alike, Sound-Alike" (LASA) drugs and dietary restrictions.

4

Visual Infographic Mapping

Instead of just text, the system maps instructions to a library of universal medical icons (e.g., a sun icon for morning dosage), ensuring the instructions are accessible at a glance.

5

Multilingual Localization

The simplified instructions are passed through a translation layer that adapts the medical guidance into over 10+ local languages, maintaining the original clinical intent.

Questions

How is patient data kept secure?

We utilize Django's built-in security middleware and implement PII (Personally Identifiable Information) scrubbing before sending data to the NLP engine.

How do you handle inaccurate OCR?

Our system includes a confidence-score threshold. If a scan is unclear, the UI prompts the user to "Review & Correct" before simplification occurs.

What happens if the AI makes a mistake in translation?

Our Safety is our priority. We implement a "Human-in-the-Loop" fallback where any low-confidence translations are flagged for pharmacist review. Additionally, we always provide the original clinical text alongside the simplified version for verification.

Does this system replace professional medical advice?

Not at all. ClarityRx is a supplemental health literacy tool. Our "Empathy-First" language engine is designed to reinforce, not replace, the specific instructions provided by a licensed healthcare professional.

How does the system handle different regional drug names?

Our Medical NLP Engine uses a semantic mapping layer. This allows us to cross-reference global generic names with local brand names to ensure accurate "Common Name" identification (e.g., Paracetamol vs. Acetaminophen).
